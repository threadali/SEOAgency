# AEO Course 2.0 — Module 8 Lecture Notes
## Off-Page Authority and Reputation

**Module tagline:** On-page perfect kiya. Ab baaki duniya ko convince karo.
**Who this is for:** Students who have built content, added schema, and are now ready to build the off-page consensus layer. This module covers the full system for making AI encounter your brand from multiple independent trusted sources — not just from your own website.

---

## L8.1 — The Consensus Principle in Depth

### Opening Hook

One doctor claims to be the best in the city. He has put a sign on his clinic wall.

Another doctor has patients writing about him. A local newspaper ran a story on his work. A medical directory lists him. His colleagues mention him by name when asked for referrals.

AI cites the second doctor. Not because his claim is stronger. Because the world confirms it independently.

This is the consensus principle. And it is the central mechanism behind all of off-page AEO.

---

### Part 1: Why One Source Is Always Just a Claim

AI systems are designed to be skeptical of single-source claims. The reason is mechanical: an LLM trained on internet data learned that websites frequently make promotional claims about themselves that are not corroborated anywhere else. A brand saying it is the best at something is a self-claim. A low-confidence input.

When AI retrieves content about a brand or a claim, it weights that content against the number of independent sources confirming the same thing. One source confirming a claim has a low weight. Three independent sources confirming the same claim has a significantly higher weight. Five or more sources in consistent language is what produces high-confidence citation.

This is not a judgment call. It is the mathematical consequence of how re-ranking models score content. Corroboration increases the re-ranking score of every retrieved passage that makes the corroborated claim.

---

### Part 2: Three Types of Consensus

**Seeded Consensus**

Seeded consensus is what you control. It is the consistent messaging you plant across your own ecosystem: website, social profiles, email signatures, team bios, press releases, partner pages, and any content you publish on other platforms.

The rule for seeded consensus: every surface where your brand appears should describe what you do in the same core language. Not creatively varied. Consistently identical on the key claims. "Goldfish and freshwater fish specialist in Lahore" is a seeded claim. If the website says this and the Google Business Profile says this and the team bio says this, the seeded consensus is strong.

Seeded consensus is the foundation. Without it, earned and third-party consensus amplify inconsistency rather than authority.

**Earned Consensus**

Earned consensus is what customers and the community say independently. Reviews, Reddit mentions, forum posts, YouTube comments, and social shares where real people use their own words to confirm what you do.

This is the most trusted type by AI because it is genuinely third-party. A customer who writes "best goldfish shop in Lahore for beginners, they walked me through everything" is providing earned consensus. It matches the seeded claim. It is independent. It uses natural customer language that closely matches how people query AI.

Earned consensus cannot be manufactured. It can only be earned by delivering value and making it easy for customers to express their experience publicly.

**Third-Party Consensus**

Third-party consensus comes from sources that are independent of your brand and independent of your customers: guest posts, press coverage, directory listings, influencer mentions, podcast appearances, and references in other businesses' content.

Third-party consensus carries different weight than earned consensus because it comes from sources with their own established authority. A mention in a credible industry publication is weighted higher than a mention in a low-authority blog. A Reddit mention in a high-karma community is weighted higher than one in a new subreddit with few members.

---

### Part 3: The Three-Step AI Validation Process

When AI encounters a query about an entity, it runs a three-step internal validation.

**Step 1: Relevancy check**
Is this entity relevant to the query? Does it operate in the right category? Does it serve the right location? Is it known for the specific angle the query asks about?

This is addressed by on-page AEO — the content, the schema, the entity establishment from Module 7.

**Step 2: Consistency check**
Does this entity say the same thing about itself across all the places AI retrieves it from? Website, social profiles, review responses, directory listings — do they all describe the same entity with the same core claims?

This is addressed by brand consistency — covered in L8.6.

**Step 3: Consensus check**
Do multiple independent sources confirm what the entity claims about itself? Are the reviews, community mentions, and third-party references aligned with the entity's own content?

This is addressed by the entire off-page AEO system in this module.

All three steps must pass for a citation to happen with high confidence. A brand that passes steps one and two but fails step three — excellent on-page, consistent messaging, but no off-page corroboration — will get cited inconsistently and on narrow queries only.

---

### Part 4: The Four-Month Consensus Timeline

Off-page consensus does not build overnight. Here is a realistic timeline.

Month one: Foundation. Consistency audit complete. Google Business Profile optimized. Top five directories updated. Entity schema live. Review collection process launched.

Month two: Earned signals begin. First batch of reviews collected. Reddit presence warming up. Quora participation begun. First guest post pitch sent.

Month three: Third-party consensus forming. First guest post published. Twenty or more Google reviews live. Reddit account making natural brand mentions in relevant threads. First PR coverage or directory inclusion with proper description.

Month four onwards: Compounding. Each citation makes the next easier. AI begins registering the brand as a consensus-backed entity rather than a self-claiming one. Citation consistency improves across platforms.

---

### Lesson Summary

- One source is always a claim. Corroboration across multiple independent sources is consensus.
- Three consensus types: seeded (you control), earned (customers independently confirm), third-party (independent sources confirm)
- AI runs a three-step validation: relevancy, consistency, consensus — all three must pass
- Consensus builds over four months, not four weeks — the timeline is predictable and manageable

## L8.2 — Google Reviews as AEO Signal

### Opening Hook

AI reads Google reviews. Not to generate summaries for humans — to understand what real customers say about a business in their own unfiltered language. That language becomes part of the evidence AI weighs when deciding whether to cite the business for specific queries.

A business with fifty reviews that describe specific outcomes, specific situations, and specific products is producing indexed, third-party, context-rich content about itself every week without writing a single word. That is what makes reviews one of the highest-leverage off-page AEO channels available.

---

### Part 1: Three Review Metrics That Matter for AEO

**Volume**
Aim for a minimum of fifty reviews for a local business. AI retrieval systems treat a thin review profile as lower authority than a substantial one. The threshold is not fifty reviews for its own sake — it is because below that number, the pattern recognition models that identify "trusted local entity" versus "unverified listing" do not trigger with high confidence.

**Recency**
Reviews from the last three months carry more weight than reviews from three years ago. AI freshness scoring applies to review platforms the same way it applies to web pages. A business with forty reviews from 2021 and no recent activity signals a potentially inactive entity. A business with forty recent reviews signals an active, currently-serving business.

Build a review collection cadence: a consistent trickle of new reviews every month rather than a burst campaign every year.

**Keyword content**
Generic reviews provide entity validation but not topical signal. "Great shop, highly recommended" tells AI the business is positively regarded but nothing about what it does or for whom.

Reviews that contain location, product type, use case, and outcome provide topical signals that directly match query patterns. "Best place to buy goldfish in Lahore if you are a first-time owner — they helped me choose the right variety for my apartment" contains five citation-relevant signals: location, product, buyer type, use case, and a specific outcome.

---

### Part 2: Engineering Better Reviews Without Incentivizing

Incentivized reviews violate Google's terms of service and create patterns that AI treats as low-trust. The right approach is systematic follow-up at the optimal moment with a specific prompt.

**The optimal moment:** Two to three weeks after purchase or service completion. Not at the point of sale. At the point where the customer has experienced a result. A goldfish bought last week may not have settled yet. A goldfish bought two weeks ago that is alive and healthy is a satisfied outcome the customer can honestly review.

**The follow-up message format:**
The prompt matters. Do not ask for a good review. Ask for a specific experience.

Message template:
"We hope your goldfish has settled in well. If you'd like to share your experience — including what you were looking for, what you found helpful, and how things are going — we'd really appreciate a Google review. It helps other buyers in the same situation find us. [link]"

This framing naturally produces the specific, context-rich language that has AEO value. It prompts the customer to describe their situation, their search context, and their outcome — exactly the content that matches query patterns.

---

### Part 3: Review Responses as Indexed AEO Content

Every response to a review is indexed text. It appears under the review in Google Maps and Google Search. AI reads it. This makes review responses a systematic content channel, not just a customer service obligation.

**The AEO-optimized review response structure:**

For positive reviews:
Acknowledge the specific thing mentioned (shows the response is genuine, not templated). Restate one core positioning claim naturally. Add one piece of expertise that reinforces the E-E-A-T signal.

Example: "Thank you for sharing this — it is exactly what we aim for. Choosing the right goldfish variety for a Lahore apartment is something we guide every first-time buyer through, because local climate conditions make a real difference to which varieties thrive here."

That response contains: location, product category, buyer type, local expertise signal, and a natural reiteration of the business's positioning. Sixty-five words. All indexed.

**For negative reviews:**
A negative review response is a larger opportunity than a positive one. Done correctly it demonstrates trustworthiness, shows a resolution process, and provides context that may correct an inaccurate impression.

Never be defensive. Never disagree publicly with the customer's experience. Acknowledge the experience, provide context if it genuinely serves the reader, and offer a resolution path.

---

### Lesson Summary

- Three review metrics: volume (minimum 50 for local), recency (consistent trickle not annual burst), keyword content (specific over generic)
- Follow-up at the optimal moment — two to three weeks post-purchase, at the point of confirmed positive outcome
- The review prompt: ask for a specific experience, not a good rating
- Review responses are indexed AEO content — write them with the same care as article content

## L8.3 — Reddit Strategy Full System

### Opening Hook

Reddit is one of the most trusted sources AI uses for community-based citations. The reasons are structural: Reddit content is unfiltered, conversational, and independent of brand interests. AI systems weight it heavily precisely because it represents what real people say when they are not trying to sell anything.

Most businesses either ignore Reddit or treat it as a minefield. Done correctly, it is one of the most cost-effective off-page AEO channels available. Done incorrectly — by attempting manufactured mentions or spam — it produces negative coverage that AI will also retrieve and cite.

---

### Part 1: Account Creation and the Warm-Up Phase

**Account creation rules:**
Do not use a username that is obviously your brand name. An account named AquaWorldLahoreOfficial will be flagged by experienced subreddit moderators immediately. Use a neutral, personal-style username.

Do not create the account and immediately start mentioning your business or posting in brand-relevant subreddits. Reddit has automated systems and experienced editors who watch for exactly this pattern. New accounts with immediate promotional behavior get banned.

Use a real email. Verify the account. Do not create multiple accounts for the same business. Reddit cross-account detection is sophisticated and coordinated promotion across accounts is a policy violation.

**The warm-up phase — six weeks:**

Weeks one and two: Make contributions that have nothing to do with your brand or business category. Answer questions you genuinely know the answers to in any subreddit. Upvote content you find useful. Post one to two helpful comments per day. The goal is edit history and karma, not visibility. An account with zero karma and zero edit history is a red flag. An account with fifty karma and two weeks of activity is a legitimate member.

Weeks three and four: Begin participating in your general topic area but not in brand-specific or directly competitive contexts. If the business is a fish shop, participate in aquarium subreddits answering genuine care questions from your knowledge — not from your product catalog.

Weeks five and six: More substantive participation. Answer questions that require genuine expertise. Share observations from your work experience without naming your business. By the end of week six, you should have thirty to fifty contributions, clean history, and an established presence as a helpful community member.

This foundation is what makes subsequent brand mentions credible rather than suspect.

---

### Part 2: Content That Reddit Values

Reddit communities value specificity, personal experience, and transparency over polished content.

A post that says "Here is what I have learned after ten years working with goldfish in Lahore — including three things most international guides get wrong" will outperform a post that reads like a reformatted blog article.

Three content formats that perform well on Reddit and produce high-quality AEO signal:

**Data posts:** Sharing specific observations or outcomes from genuine experience. "We tracked 200 goldfish purchases over the last year — here is what we found about first-week survival rates and the three things that made the difference." Specific numbers, genuine data, transparent methodology.

**Mistake posts:** "The three most common goldfish mistakes I see at our shop and how to avoid them." These attract engagement because they are immediately useful and they address the pre-awareness stage query that many Reddit users are experiencing right now.

**Comparison posts:** "Common Goldfish vs Fantail — which one is actually easier to keep in a Pakistani apartment?" Genuine comparison based on real experience. Not promotional. Not pushing a product. Just answering a question AI cannot answer well from training data alone.

---

### Part 3: Engineering Organic Brand Mentions

The most powerful Reddit AEO signal is an unprompted recommendation from a third-party account. You cannot manufacture this directly — but you can create conditions that make it more likely.

Make it easy for satisfied customers to find relevant subreddits. When customers ask where they can recommend you online, mention Reddit alongside Google. Some customers are active Reddit users and will naturally share good experiences in the right community if they know it exists.

Identify the subreddits where your potential customers ask for recommendations. Monitor them. When someone asks for a recommendation that your business fits, a satisfied customer who already knows about the community can mention you naturally. You cannot plant that comment yourself — but you can create the customer relationships and community awareness that makes it happen organically.

A single unprompted recommendation in a high-traffic thread with fifty upvotes can produce AI citations on that specific query for years because Reddit threads do not disappear and AI retrieval systems index them continuously.

---

### Part 4: What Not to Do

Do not create multiple accounts to manufacture brand mentions. Reddit's detection systems identify coordinated behavior through timing patterns, language patterns, and IP overlap. Manufactured mentions get removed, the accounts get banned, and the thread may generate negative discussion about the manipulation attempt — which AI will also index and cite.

Do not respond aggressively or defensively to negative mentions of your brand. Reddit communities take the side of the community member over the business in disputes. A defensive brand response amplifies the negative thread by generating more engagement. One calm, solution-focused response maximum. After that, silence is better than escalation.

Do not remove posts or comments once made. Deleted content with visible edit history attracts more attention than the original comment. If something you posted was wrong or inappropriate, address it in a follow-up comment, do not delete.

---

### Lesson Summary

- Reddit is weighted heavily by AI because it is independent of brand interests
- Six-week warm-up: two weeks general contributions, two weeks topic-adjacent participation, two weeks substantive expertise sharing
- Three high-performing content formats: data posts, mistake posts, comparison posts
- Organic mentions from third-party accounts are the most powerful signal — create the conditions, not the mentions
- Coordinated accounts, defensive responses, and deleted content all produce negative signals

## L8.4 — Backlink Strategy for AEO

### Opening Hook

In traditional SEO, a link was a vote. More votes, higher ranking. The quantity and domain authority of the linking site were the primary signals.

In AEO, the signal has shifted. A link in a relevant article from a trusted source, in a sentence that explicitly describes your specific expertise in the context of your target topic, is worth more than fifty directory links or a hundred generic mentions. The context surrounding the link is what AI reads. The link itself confirms the relationship.

---

### Part 1: What Makes a Link AEO-Valuable

An AEO-valuable link contains three elements in the surrounding context:

**Entity recognition:** The name of the business is mentioned in a way that confirms it as a real entity in a specific category. "AquaWorld" mentioned in passing provides entity recognition. "AquaWorld, a Lahore-based freshwater fish specialist" provides entity recognition with category and location confirmation.

**Topical alignment:** The link appears within an article on a topic directly relevant to the linked business's expertise. A link to a goldfish shop appearing in an article about goldfish care in Pakistan is topically aligned. The same link appearing in an article about general pet ownership is less aligned. Both exist. Both help. The aligned one helps more.

**Specific claim:** The sentence containing the link makes a specific claim about the business. "AquaWorld is one of the few shops in Lahore that specifically stocks cold-water goldfish varieties suited to Pakistani climate conditions" is a specific claim. "AquaWorld sells goldfish" is not.

A link with all three elements in the surrounding context produces a stronger AEO signal than domain authority alone.

---

### Part 2: Guest Post Strategy for AEO Context

The goal of an AEO guest post is not the link. The goal is the surrounding context.

Before pitching a guest post, identify the specific claim about your business that you want to establish in third-party content. Not "we are good at goldfish." Something specific: "AquaWorld is the only shop in Lahore that specifically advises on coldwater goldfish varieties suitable for Pakistani climate conditions."

Write the guest post article to establish that claim. The article is genuinely useful to the publication's audience — it covers a specific topic in depth. Naturally, in the body of the article, the business appears as a reference that supports the specific claim.

The result: a third-party publication on a relevant topic, written by an expert, containing a specific factual claim about your business, with a link confirming the relationship. That is double corroboration from one piece of content.

**Outreach approach:**
Target publications in your niche with real audiences. Not high-DA farms. Niche publications with engaged readerships that your ICP actually reads.

Pitch an article on a topic where you have genuine expertise and original information to share. Not a thinly veiled advertisement. A piece that the editor's audience would value and share.

The guest post that gets published because it is genuinely good produces a citation-quality link. The guest post that barely passes because it was clearly promotional produces a link that AI reads in a low-trust context.

---

### Part 3: Broken Link Building for AEO

Broken link building is underused in AEO and works for the same reason it works in SEO — except the value in AEO comes from the replacement context, not just the link.

Process:
Search for resource pages or articles in your niche that link to content no longer available. Use tools like Ahrefs site explorer or simply search for key topics and check external links on the results.

When you find a broken link to a resource that no longer exists, create the replacement resource. Make it better than the original — more current, more specific, better structured.

Contact the site and let them know: the resource they linked to no longer exists, and here is a current replacement on the same topic. Offer the replacement link.

This works because you are solving a real problem for the site owner. The success rate is higher than cold outreach pitches. And the replacement link is in context-rich surrounding content that already existed to support the original resource — meaning the AEO value is pre-validated by the original article's existence.

---

### Part 4: Unlinked Mentions and Link Velocity

**Unlinked mentions still matter.** A brand mention without a hyperlink still registers as a consensus signal in AI retrieval systems. When a journalist writes about the best goldfish shops in Lahore and mentions AquaWorld without linking to the website, AI still reads the mention in context and adds it to entity recognition. Pursue linked mentions where possible. Do not dismiss unlinked coverage as worthless.

**Link velocity:** Build two to five high-quality contextual links per month for most businesses. More than that risks unnatural pattern detection. Fewer than that means the authority signal compounds slowly.

The practical priority: one guest post per month in a genuinely relevant publication, plus one local or directory citation update, plus whatever earned coverage naturally occurs. That cadence over six months produces a measurable AEO impact without triggering quality review flags.

---

### Lesson Summary

- AEO-valuable links have three contextual elements: entity recognition, topical alignment, specific claim
- Guest posts target context first, link second — pitch genuinely useful articles that naturally reference the business
- Broken link building works because it solves a real problem — success rate is higher than cold outreach
- Unlinked mentions contribute to entity recognition — pursue them alongside linked coverage
- Two to five quality links per month is a sustainable velocity for most businesses

## L8.5 — Off-Site Consolidation

### Opening Hook

Off-page AEO is not just about creating new mentions. It is also about making sure every existing mention across the internet is accurate, consistent, and contributes to the right entity signal. Most businesses have fragmented off-site presence without realizing it — inconsistent directory listings, outdated descriptions, profiles that describe a version of the business from three years ago.

Each inconsistency is a point where AI encounters a conflicting signal about your entity. The confidence score for every claim involving your brand drops slightly each time that happens.

---

### Part 1: Business Directories

The major directories — Google Business Profile, Yelp, Bing Places, Apple Maps, Foursquare, and any industry-specific directories relevant to your niche — should all have identical NAP (name, address, phone) and consistent business description and category.

Audit these once per quarter. Run a search for your business name in Google. Click every result that lists the business. Verify the name, address, phone number, description, and category on every platform matches the values in your website schema.

The most common inconsistencies: different versions of the business name (AquaWorld vs. Aqua World vs. AquaWorld Lahore), different phone number formats, outdated addresses from previous locations, descriptions that describe a previous version of the business before a pivot or rebranding.

Fix every inconsistency before adding new off-page channels. New mentions added on top of an inconsistent foundation amplify the inconsistency, not the authority.

---

### Part 2: Quora

Quora has high domain authority in AI retrieval systems and its answers frequently appear in AI responses to research-oriented queries. A well-written Quora answer establishing genuine expertise that naturally references your brand can produce AI citations for years.

The same warm-up principle applies as Reddit: build a profile with real information, answer questions across your topic area genuinely before mentioning your brand, and let the brand mention appear naturally after context is established.

Quora answers that look like advertisements get downvoted and lose authority quickly. Quora answers that provide specific, useful, non-promotional answers to questions that remain permanently searchable produce the most durable off-page AEO value.

---

### Part 3: YouTube Transcripts as Indexed Content

YouTube video transcripts are indexed by AI retrieval systems. A video answering a specific question in your topic area produces a transcript that AI can retrieve and use in citations. The spoken content becomes indexed text. Video descriptions function as links. Comments from viewers add community signal.

You do not need a large YouTube channel for this to work. A single well-produced video answering a specific O-grade query question in your topic area — with a descriptive title, an accurate auto-generated or manually added transcript, and a relevant description — can produce citations on that specific query indefinitely.

The transcript quality matters. Automatically generated transcripts are often inaccurate for technical terms or non-English words. A manually corrected transcript ensures the indexed text accurately represents what was said.

---

### Part 4: Podcast and Interview Appearances

Every podcast episode or video interview transcript containing your name and your topic-relevant expertise is an indexed piece of off-site content. AI retrieval systems reach these transcripts.

A podcast appearance where you discuss goldfish care in Pakistani climate conditions, say your business name, and provide expert guidance on a specific constrained topic produces:
- An indexed audio-to-text transcript containing your name and expertise claims
- A show notes page often containing a brief description and a link
- A third-party publication (the podcast platform's website) confirming your expert status

Three off-page signals from one appearance. The time investment of thirty to sixty minutes for a podcast interview produces more AEO value per hour than most other off-page activities.

---

### Lesson Summary

- Quarterly directory audit: verify NAP consistency across all platforms
- Quora answers: build authority before mentioning brand, answer genuinely in topic area
- YouTube transcripts: one video on an O-grade query topic can produce durable citations
- Podcast appearances: transcript + show notes + third-party expert confirmation = three signals per appearance
- Fix consistency before adding new channels — new mentions amplify what is already there

## L8.6 — Brand Consistency Audit and AI Mirror Test

### Opening Hook

Everything in this module builds on a foundation: consistent brand messaging across every surface where the brand appears. Without that foundation, off-page activity amplifies noise rather than signal.

The brand consistency audit runs before any new off-page activity begins. The AI mirror test runs monthly to check whether the off-page work is producing the right brand description in AI responses.

---

### Part 1: The Brand Consistency Audit

Run this audit before starting any new off-page channel and quarterly thereafter.

**The seven fields to check:**

**Field 1: Business name format**
Is the exact business name — including capitalization, spacing, and any descriptors — identical across website, Google Business Profile, all directories, social profiles, and schema markup?

**Field 2: Core speciality claim**
The one or two sentences describing what the business does. Is this identical in substance across all platforms? Not word-for-word identical everywhere, but the core claim — the specific thing the business specializes in — should be the same.

**Field 3: Location language**
For local businesses: is the service area described consistently? "Lahore" on some platforms and "Lahore, Pakistan" on others is a minor inconsistency. "Lahore" on some and "Punjab" on others is a more significant one. Standardize to the most specific description consistently.

**Field 4: Author and team bios**
If team members appear in external content — guest posts, podcast bios, LinkedIn profiles — do their credentials and affiliations match what is stated on the company website?

**Field 5: Schema description**
Does the description in the Organization or LocalBusiness schema match the description used in the Google Business Profile and directory listings?

**Field 6: External press mentions**
Are there any press mentions or directory listings that describe an outdated version of the business — before a pivot, name change, or specialty shift? These need to be corrected or flagged. They are active sources of wrong AI entity descriptions.

**Field 7: Social profile descriptions**
All social profiles should describe the same business with the same core claim. A Facebook page that says "aquarium supplies" and a website that says "goldfish specialist" are describing different businesses to AI.

---

### Part 2: The AI Mirror Test

The AI mirror test is a monthly diagnostic that checks whether AI's current description of your brand matches your intended positioning.

**The test protocol:**

Run the same three prompts across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overview.

Prompt 1: "What do you know about [Business Name]?"
Prompt 2: "What is [Business Name] known for?"
Prompt 3: "Who should use [Business Name] and why?"

Write down the exact descriptions you receive. Compare them to:
- The positioning you want
- The positioning that appears in your schema
- The positioning that appears in your Google Business Profile

Three types of results and what each means:

**Result: Description matches intended positioning**
Off-page consensus is working. Seeded, earned, and third-party signals are aligned. Continue building volume and consistency.

**Result: Description is incomplete — missing a key positioning element**
A specific element of your positioning has insufficient external corroboration. The element is in your own content but not yet confirmed by enough independent sources. Identify which off-page channels are weakest for that specific claim and prioritize them.

**Result: Description is wrong — contains inaccurate claims**
Identify the source. Is it an outdated directory listing? A press article with an error? A Wikipedia or Wikidata entry that is incorrect? The wrong description can only be corrected by fixing the source. You cannot correct AI directly — you correct the inputs it retrieves from.

---

### Part 3: Fixing Wrong AI Descriptions

When AI gives a wrong description of your brand, the fix is a three-step process.

**Step 1: Source identification**
Search for the specific wrong claim in quotes in Google. Find which indexed page is the source of the inaccurate information. Check whether it appears in your schema, your Google Business Profile, a directory listing, or a press article.

**Step 2: Source correction**
Correct the source directly:
- Schema: edit and republish with correct information
- Google Business Profile: update through the Business Profile dashboard
- Directories: log in and update, or contact the directory if you cannot
- Press articles: reach out to the publication with the correct information
- Wikipedia or Wikidata: use the talk page process for Wikipedia, direct edit for Wikidata

**Step 3: New signal reinforcement**
After correcting the source, publish new content that contains the correct description. A new article, a new FAQ answer, a new review response — all containing the accurate claim. AI's description updates as it re-indexes corrected sources and encounters new consistent signals.

Timeline for AI mirror test improvement after a fix: Perplexity typically reflects changes within two to four weeks. Google AI Overview takes four to eight weeks. ChatGPT base model updates with training cycles and may take longer. Use Perplexity as your earliest feedback signal.

---

### Lesson Summary

- Brand consistency audit: seven fields, run before any new off-page activity and quarterly thereafter
- AI mirror test: three prompts across three platforms, monthly
- Three result types: matching (continue building), incomplete (target weak channel), wrong (fix the source)
- Source correction precedes new signal reinforcement — fix what is wrong before adding more of the right
- Perplexity as fastest feedback signal, two to four weeks after a correction

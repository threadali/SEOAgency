### Step 0B: Current Reality Check (May 2026)

These are confirmed facts as of the project start date. Update if evidence changes.

**Zero-Click Reality:**
- Over 60–65% of searches end without a click (Search Engine Land, Aug 2025)
- When Google AI Overview appears, clicks drop 58% from position 1 (Ahrefs, Dec 2025)
- AI-driven visitors convert at 4.4x the rate of standard organic visitors (Frase.io, 2025)
- AI referral sessions to websites grew 527% year-over-year through mid-2025 (Frase.io, 2025)

**Google's Official AEO Stance (May 2026):**
- Google calls AEO/GEO "still SEO" — not a separate discipline for Google properties
- Google says chunking content is NOT required — their systems handle multi-topic pages
- Google says llms.txt files have NO special treatment
- Google says foundational SEO (crawlability, helpfulness, E-E-A-T) drives AI citation
- Non-commodity content ("Why We Waived the Inspection") outperforms commodity content ("7 Tips")
- AI-assisted content is fine if it meets E-E-A-T and is not produced to manipulate rankings

**What This Means Practically:**
- Write for humans first. Google AI cites what humans find useful.
- Do NOT chunk artificially. Write coherent, full-coverage sections.
- DO use structured data (FAQPage, Article, HowTo) — this helps Google confirm what pages are about.
- DO focus on original data and unique insight — this is what all AEO platforms cite.
- Non-Google platforms (ChatGPT, Perplexity) have independent citation logic — optimize for both.

## PART 3: AUTOMATED PRE-WRITING RESEARCH

Claude performs the following before drafting. This is non-optional.

### 3A: Competitor Gap Analysis

1. Search the primary keyword on Google, ChatGPT, and Perplexity
2. Pull the top 3–5 ranking pages / cited sources
3. Read each for: what angle they cover, what they miss, what they get wrong
4. Identify the content gap — the angle that no competitor covers
5. This gap becomes the Information Gain section of the article

### 3B: Fan-Out Query Mapping

Every main query fans out into sub-queries when an AI processes it.
Map all sub-queries before writing. Each one = a potential H2 section.

Fan-out example for "goldfish summer care Lahore":
- What temperature kills goldfish?
- How to cool a fish tank without AC?
- What to do during power outage with fish?
- Which goldfish survive high temperatures?
- How often to change water in summer?
- Can I use ice in a fish tank?

Each sub-query becomes an H2. Each H2 = a citation opportunity.

Tools to use:
- Cuporia by Mike King (free fan-out extraction): https://cuporia.com
- AlsoAsked: https://alsoasked.com
- Google People Also Ask (manual check)
- ChatGPT: "List all sub-questions someone might ask about [topic]"

### 3C: Citable Source Identification

For every major factual claim, find a citable source. Prioritize:
1. Google Search Central (any claim about how Google works)
2. Peer-reviewed research or government data
3. Named industry reports (Moz, Ahrefs, HubSpot data)
4. Verified customer data from the business itself
5. Reddit / community forums — for customer language only, not citations

Flag: any claim without a source must be marked as business-owned data or removed.

## PART 4: GOOGLE COMPLIANCE RULES — NON-NEGOTIABLE

Every article must pass these before publishing. Violations risk deindexing.

### Spam Policy Compliance (Google, updated Dec 2025)

| Rule | What It Means for This Project |
|---|---|
| No scaled content abuse | Each article must have unique, original insight. No spinning. |
| No thin affiliate content | Product mentions must add context, not just drop links. |
| No cloaking | Content shown to Google must be identical to content shown to users. |
| No link schemes | Internal links must be editorially placed — not for rank manipulation. |
| No site reputation abuse | No publishing articles designed solely to borrow domain authority. |
| No back button hijacking | Do not use redirect tricks or popups that break browser navigation. |
| AI content must meet E-E-A-T | AI-assisted writing is fine. AI-stuffed ranking manipulation is a violation. |

### E-E-A-T Requirements (Every Article)

E-E-A-T = Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness.
Google's quality raters evaluate pages against this. AI citation systems use the same signals.

- **Experience**: First-hand experience must be present. Use real customer stories, real data, real local context. Not hypothetical.
- **Expertise**: Content written at the level of someone who does this daily. No surface-level explanation of basics.
- **Authoritativeness**: Cite named sources. Reference real data. Link to reputable external sources.
- **Trustworthiness**: Accurate claims only. Include guardrails (when the advice does NOT apply). Never overclaim.

Practical implementation:
- Every article must include at least one original data point or verifiable business-specific claim
- Author bio or business attribution required on published page
- External links to authoritative sources (not competitors) in every article
- Guardrail statement in every major section and every FAQ answer

### Helpful Content Standards

Google's own test: "Would a visitor feel satisfied after reading this?"
Apply it to every H2 section individually. If the answer is no — rewrite or cut.

Do NOT write:
- Content that exists only to match a keyword pattern
- Sections that repeat what's in a previous section without adding new information
- Intros that describe what the article will cover instead of covering it
- Conclusions that summarize what was just said

## PART 5: WRITING SYSTEM — FULL PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: Brief

Use the brief template from the original AEO system (v1.0).
Add these fields:

```
INFORMATION_GAIN_SOURCE:  [Where is the unique data coming from?]
CITABLE_CLAIMS:           [List 3 claims this article makes that no competitor makes]
E-E-A-T_SIGNALS:          [How will experience/expertise show up in this article?]
GOOGLE_COMPLIANCE_CHECK:  [Any spam risk flags? Addressed how?]
```

### Phase 2: TL;DR

Rules unchanged from v1.0. Additional requirement:
- At least one TL;DR bullet must contain a verifiable data point (number, percentage, timeframe, or named study)

### Phase 3: AQCE Structure

Rules unchanged from v1.0. Additional requirements:

**Answer (A):**
- First sentence must be answerable as a voice response. Read it aloud. If it sounds natural as an AI assistant speaking it — it works.
- No longer than 40 words for the opening sentence.

**Qualify (Q):**
- Second paragraph must contain at least one external citation or one business-owned data point.
- Third paragraph must acknowledge a constraint, exception, or limitation. This is the guardrail paragraph.

**Cover (C):**
- Every H2 heading must pass the Taco Bell Test AND the Voice Test (works read aloud as a standalone answer).
- Minimum heading depth: H1 > H2 > H3. Use H4 for tables, comparisons, and sub-point breakdowns.
- Each H2 section should include: mini-answer, mechanism explanation, proof, guardrail.

**Extend (E):**
- Minimum 5 FAQs. Maximum 12.
- 70/30 split: 70% generic topic FAQs, 30% brand-specific FAQs.
- Product tie-in must appear naturally in at least 3 FAQ answers.

### Phase 4: Information Gain

One of these three must appear in every article:

**Type 1 — Original Local/Business Data:**
Exact format: "[Business name] data from [timeframe] shows [specific metric]."
Example: "RankArrow client data from Q1 2025 shows pages with FAQ schema received 34% more AI Overview appearances than pages without."

**Type 2 — Customer Story:**
Exact format: "[Customer descriptor] — [location] — [situation]. [Action taken]. [Specific result with number or timeframe]."
Example: "A Lahore-based ecommerce founder using RankArrow's AEO framework saw AI-referred sessions grow from 0 to 340/month in 60 days without changing ad spend."

**Type 3 — After-Reading Gap:**
Find the question readers have after reading competitor articles.
Search: Reddit, Quora, "still confused after reading about [topic]"
Create a dedicated H2 section that answers exactly that question.
Label it clearly: "What Most [Topic] Guides Miss: [Specific Gap]"

### Phase 5: Human Writing Pass

Apply the Human Writing Cleaner skill to every section after drafting.

Rules:
- Remove all em dashes (—) → rewrite using comma or period
- Remove: "leverage", "utilize", "ensure", "furthermore", "seamless", "robust", "game-changer"
- Remove: rhetorical questions as section openers
- Remove: "In today's world / landscape / environment" openers
- Remove: vague sentence starters like "This means that..."
- Every sentence must earn its place — if removing it loses no meaning, cut it
- Paragraphs longer than 4 lines must be split or tightened

### Phase 6: Product Tie-In (Tactic 7 — MOST IMPORTANT)

Every article must organically mention the brand as a solution.
This is the mechanism by which AI learns to recommend the business.

Rules:
- Mention appears in every second H2 section at minimum
- Product mention is factual — it describes what the product actually does
- Mention follows the one-two punch: [Insight] → [How brand addresses this]
- Never force the mention. If a section has no natural connection, skip it and catch the next one.
- Decision-stage sections must have the clearest, most direct product connection

Banned phrases for product mentions:
- "Check out..."
- "Learn more about..."
- "Our amazing..."
- "Best-in-class..."

Allowed structures:
- "[Brand] does this by..."
- "Teams using [Brand] have found..."
- "[Brand]'s [specific feature] addresses this because..."
- "[Brand] client data shows..."

## PART 6: HEADING STRUCTURE PROTOCOL

### Maximum Heading Depth

Use every level where it adds clarity. Do not skip levels.

```
H1 — Article title (one per page)
H2 — Major sections (each = one fan-out query answered)
H3 — Sub-sections within H2 (mechanism, proof, guardrail, example)
H4 — Tables, comparisons, lists with multiple attributes
H5 — Optional: step breakdowns within H3s
```

### H2 Heading Rules

Every H2 must:
- Be phrased as a question
- Include a constraint, location, or persona qualifier where relevant
- Be answerable without reading the rest of the article (Taco Bell Test)
- Be answerable as a voice response (Voice Test)

Bad → Good examples:
- "Temperature Management" → "What Temperature Kills Goldfish in Lahore's Summer Heat?"
- "Our Solution" → "How RankArrow Builds AEO Content That Gets Cited by ChatGPT"
- "Tips for Beginners" → "What Should First-Time Goldfish Owners Do in Pakistan's Summer?"

### H3 Heading Rules

H3 heads go inside H2 sections. Use them for:
- Mechanism explanation ("Why This Happens")
- The proof block ("What the Data Shows")
- The guardrail ("When This Doesn't Apply")
- Step-by-step sub-sections ("Step 1: ...", "Step 2: ...")

## PART 7: SCHEMA MARKUP PROTOCOL

### Required Schema Per Page Type

| Page Type | Required Schema |
|---|---|
| Every article | Article schema + FAQPage schema |
| Home page | Organization OR LocalBusiness schema |
| Product / service page | Product schema OR Service schema |
| How-to guide | HowTo schema |
| Comparison page | ItemList schema |
| Local page | LocalBusiness + BreadcrumbList |

### Schema Compliance Rules (Google, updated Jan 2026)

- Schema text must match visible page text exactly. Mismatch = manual action risk.
- Never mark up content that is not visible to users.
- Do not use schema to misrepresent page content.
- Validate every schema with Google Rich Results Test before publishing: https://search.google.com/test/rich-results
- Never publish schema with errors. Broken schema is worse than no schema.
- Do not block schema pages from Googlebot via robots.txt or noindex.

## PART 11: THE 3 PILLARS OF AEO — MASTER REFERENCE

Every article must satisfy all three. Check each before finalising.

### Pillar 1 — Build Consensus

AI engines look for the same information across multiple sources before citing it as fact.
Implementation:
- Use the same core claims, statistics, and positioning in all published articles
- Ensure the same messaging appears on social media, review platforms, and partner mentions
- Consistent brand description across G2, Capterra, LinkedIn, Reddit

### Pillar 2 — Provide Information Gain

AI cites content that provides net-new information not already in its training data.
Implementation:
- Original business data > industry reports > general knowledge
- Customer stories with specific numbers always outperform general claims
- After-reading gaps (things competitors don't cover) = guaranteed citation opportunities

### Pillar 3 — Clear Semantic Structure

AI engines are pattern-matching systems. Messy structure = missed citations.
Implementation:
- Question-format H2s
- Short paragraphs (2–3 sentences)
- Short sentences (under 20 words)
- Tables for comparisons
- FAQ section at bottom of every article
- FAQPage schema validating the structure

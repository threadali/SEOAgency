# MODULE 7 — Topical Map Construction
## Core Topics + Outer Topics + Links to CMap

> **Purpose of this module:** The CMap defined your site's skeleton. The Topical Map fills it with content. Every article, every supporting guide, every comparison piece, every intersection article your site will ever publish — it is planned here, before any brief is written. The Topical Map is not a content calendar. It is a complete allocation of every query group in your keyword spreadsheet to a specific page, with a specific macro context, a specific parent in the CMap, and a specific position in the authority-building sequence. When it is complete, your writers receive only two things: a topical map entry and a content brief. They never decide what to write. The map decides.

---

## LESSON 7.1 — Topical Map vs Conceptual Map

### The Critical Distinction

Before building the Topical Map, understand what it is not.

A **Conceptual Map** imitates what is already appearing on the SERP. It looks at the top 10 results for your target queries and plans articles that cover the same ground — the same topics, the same angles, the same structure. Conceptual maps produce average content. They produce content that is the same as everything already indexed, which is exactly what Google does not need more of.

A **Topical Map** changes the perception of your website in Google's eyes and in the user's eyes. It is built from your Source Context, Central Entity, and Central Search Intent — not from competitor SERP analysis. It covers what the entity actually demands based on the knowledge territory (Mind Map) rather than what competitors happen to have written.

**The difference in outcome:**

| Conceptual Map Approach | Topical Map Approach |
|---|---|
| Copies competitor topic coverage | Maps complete entity knowledge territory |
| Produces average, undifferentiated content | Produces unique, authority-building content |
| Ranks somewhere in the middle | Builds the site Google treats as the authority |
| Plateau after initial gains | Compounds over time as topical authority grows |
| Ignores underserved user segments | Deliberately serves every user group |
| Built from SERP analysis | Built from entity research + source context |

**The topical map's job is to make your site the most complete, most structured, most useful treatment of your Central Entity on the internet within your Source Context.**

## LESSON 7.2 — The Architecture: Core Section and Outer Section

### Recap from Module 1 — Now Applied to Article Planning

In Module 1, Core and Outer were introduced as abstract concepts. In the Topical Map, they are the fundamental structural divisions that determine where every article sits, what depth it achieves, and how it connects to everything else.

```
CORE SECTION  =  Central Entity  +  Source Context  (direct attributes)
OUTER SECTION =  Central Entity  +  Predicates of Central Search Intent (peripheral attributes)
```

### Core Section: What It Is

The Core Section is the set of articles that directly cover the primary attributes of your Central Entity as defined by your Source Context. These are the articles that build topical authority — they are the reason Google eventually trusts your entire site.

**Characteristics of Core section articles:**

| Property | Core Section |
|---|---|
| Topic source | Root attributes from Entity Map (all three criteria pass) |
| Content depth | Maximum — all values, all sub-types, complete EAV coverage |
| Information gain | Mandatory — must exceed competitors on at least 2–3 points |
| Crawl priority | Closest to homepage after CMap pillar pages |
| Link direction | Receives links from Outer section articles |
| Quality standard | Highest — expert, specific, data-rich, no filler |
| Publishing priority | First — Core articles before any Outer articles |

**Core section articles for a Pakistani heater retailer:**
```
→ Gas Heater Types in Pakistan (entity: gas heater, attribute: type)
→ Gas Heater Wattage Guide — Room Size Calculator (attribute: wattage)
→ Gas Heater Safety Guide Pakistan (attribute: safety)
→ Gas Heater Energy Cost Pakistan (attribute: fuel consumption)
→ Gas Heater Maintenance Guide Pakistan (attribute: maintenance)
→ Gas Heater Carbon Monoxide Risk Guide (attribute: safety/CO)
→ LPG vs Natural Gas Heater Pakistan (attribute: fuel type)
→ Best Gas Heater Brands Pakistan — Full Comparison (attribute: brand)
```

Each of these directly covers one entity-attribute pair at maximum depth. Each is an expert authority piece. Each earns its place by scoring on all three evaluation criteria.

### Outer Section: What It Is

The Outer Section is the set of articles that expand query coverage beyond the Core. These articles cover the predicates of the Central Search Intent — the peripheral attributes, the edge cases, the specific user sub-segments, the adjacent topics that connect back to the Central Entity without being a root attribute.

**Characteristics of Outer section articles:**

| Property | Outer Section |
|---|---|
| Topic source | Rare attributes + Central Search Intent predicates |
| Content depth | Flat — cover the topic adequately, do not go deep |
| Information gain | Useful but not mandatory at the same level as Core |
| Crawl priority | Deeper in hierarchy — after Core articles are indexed |
| Link direction | Links OUT to Core section articles |
| Quality standard | Good — accurate, useful, but lighter than Core |
| Publishing priority | After Core section is established |

**Outer section articles for a Pakistani heater retailer:**
```
→ Gas Heater with Remote Control in Pakistan (rare attribute: remote)
→ Smart Gas Heater WiFi Models Pakistan (rare attribute: smart connectivity)
→ Gas Heater for Small Apartments Pakistan (user segment: apartment dwellers)
→ Gas Heater for Elderly and Infants Safety Tips (user segment: safety-sensitive)
→ Gas Heater vs Oil-Filled Heater: Which to Buy? (adjacent comparison)
→ How to Buy a Heater Online in Pakistan (process: transaction support)
→ Gas Heater Spare Parts in Pakistan (post-purchase peripheral)
→ When to Replace Your Gas Heater (post-purchase peripheral)
```

These are real user needs — but they are not the core of what a heater retailer's authority is built on. They expand the query surface area and capture session variety. Critically, each one links back to a Core section article.

### The Structural Rule

```
Core Section → builds topical authority (depth strategy)
Outer Section → expands query surface (width strategy)

Outer links to Core — always.
Core never links exclusively to Outer.
```

A Core section article is the destination. An Outer section article is the pathway that leads users and crawlers back to the Core.

## LESSON 7.3 — Topical Map Dimensions: Vast, Depth, Momentum

### The Three Dimensions That Define Your Strategy

Every topical map has three dimensions that define its character. Understanding these dimensions allows you to make deliberate strategic decisions — not just pile up articles.

### Dimension 1 — Vast (Width)

**Definition:** How many distinct topics you cover in the Outer Section. Width is the horizontal spread of your query coverage.

A **wide** topical map covers many peripheral topics — multiple user segments, many adjacent comparisons, broad post-purchase coverage. It captures a large variety of user sessions.

A **narrow** topical map concentrates on the Core Section only, with minimal Outer coverage.

**When to go wide:** When your Core Section is already well-established (published, indexed, ranking), you have the content production capacity, and there is genuine search demand across many Outer topics.

**When to stay narrow:** When starting out, when production resources are limited, or when the Outer topics are too low-volume to justify the investment. Start narrow, go deep, then expand.

### Dimension 2 — Depth (Height)

**Definition:** How many levels of supporting articles exist beneath each Core pillar. Depth is the vertical depth of your authority on specific attributes.

A **deep** topical map has multiple supporting articles beneath each pillar page — covering sub-attributes, specific user segments, model-specific content, and edge cases for each root attribute.

A **shallow** topical map has one or two supporting articles per pillar at most.

**When to go deep:** When competing in a high-authority niche where surface-level Core content is not enough. When the attribute branch has high density (many viable sub-topics) as identified in the Mind Map.

**When to stay shallow initially:** When the niche is new to your domain, when pillar pages themselves are not yet ranking, or when the sub-attribute query volumes don't justify dedicated pages.

### Dimension 3 — Momentum (Speed)

**Definition:** How fast you publish and expand the topical map. Momentum is the publication velocity.

**High momentum:** Publishing multiple articles per week. Forces more systematic brief production. Requires tight quality control to prevent rushed content from diluting E-E-A-T.

**Low momentum:** Publishing one to two articles per week. Allows deeper research and higher per-article quality. Slower authority accumulation but lower risk.

**The strategic rule:**
```
If you cannot go wider → go deeper and faster
Go wide only when you can also go deep on specific attributes
Never sacrifice depth for width
Never sacrifice quality for momentum
```

**The momentum trap:** High momentum with low quality is worse than low momentum with high quality. A site that publishes 30 thin Outer section articles before establishing 5 strong Core articles is building authority in the wrong direction. Google evaluates the Core articles first. If they are weak, the Outer articles contribute nothing.

## LESSON 7.4 — Topic Derivation: Where Every Article Comes From

Every article in the Topical Map is derived from one of five sources. Every source traces back to prior research documents. No article is invented — every article answers a documented query need.

### Source 1 — Root Attribute Coverage (Core Section)

**Origin:** Layer 2 branches of the Mind Map (root attributes)
**Rule:** Every root attribute that scores on all three criteria (Prominent, Popular, Relevant) generates at least one Core section article.
**Result:** The foundational Core section articles — one per qualifying root attribute.

```
Root attribute: Wattage → Core article: Gas Heater Wattage Guide Pakistan
Root attribute: Safety → Core article: Gas Heater Safety and Indoor Use Guide
Root attribute: Price → Core article: Gas Heater Price List Pakistan 2025
Root attribute: Types → Core article: Gas Heater Types in Pakistan — Complete Guide
Root attribute: Brands → Core article: Best Gas Heater Brands Pakistan — Comparison
Root attribute: Fuel Type → Core article: LPG vs Natural Gas Heater Pakistan
```

### Source 2 — Value Cluster Expansion (Core Section, Depth)

**Origin:** Layer 3 value clusters in the Mind Map that are large enough to require a dedicated page
**Rule:** When a value cluster has 3+ distinct sub-values each with their own query volume, that cluster warrants a dedicated supporting article beneath the relevant pillar.
**Result:** Deep supporting articles beneath Core pillars.

```
Root attribute: Types → Value cluster: Gas Heater sub-types
  → Supporting: Blue Flame Gas Heater — How It Works + Pakistan Price
  → Supporting: Infrared Gas Heater Pakistan — Direct Heat Explained
  → Supporting: Catalytic Gas Heater — Safest Type for Indoors?

Root attribute: Brands → Value cluster: Individual Pakistani brands
  → Supporting: Gree Gas Heater Pakistan — All Models, Specs, Prices
  → Supporting: Orient Gas Heater Pakistan — Review + Price Guide
  → Supporting: Dawlance Gas Heater Pakistan — Full Review
```

### Source 3 — Query Path Coverage (Core + Outer)

**Origin:** Layer 4 query overlays from the Mind Map — query path stages that need dedicated coverage
**Rule:** Each distinct query path stage with sufficient volume that isn't already covered by a root attribute article gets its own Outer section article, linked to the relevant Core article.
**Result:** Articles covering specific stages of the user journey not captured by root attribute coverage.

```
Query path stage: Post-purchase maintenance
  → Outer article: Gas Heater Maintenance Guide Pakistan
  → Links to: Gas Heater Types pillar, Gas Heater Brands pillar

Query path stage: Troubleshooting
  → Outer article: Gas Heater Not Working — Common Problems + Fixes
  → Links to: Gas Heater Safety pillar, relevant brand pillar

Query path stage: Purchase process support
  → Outer article: How to Buy a Gas Heater Online in Pakistan
  → Links to: Gas Heater Price pillar, Gas Heater Brands pillar
```

### Source 4 — Related Entity Intersection (Outer Section)

**Origin:** Layer 5 related entity connections from the Mind Map
**Rule:** Every documented related entity connection with a corresponding query cluster generates one intersection article.
**Result:** Articles that bridge two entities, capturing users navigating between adjacent query clusters.

```
Gas Heater ──[creates risk of]──→ Carbon Monoxide
  → Intersection: Gas Heater Carbon Monoxide Safety — What Pakistani Homeowners Must Know
  → Links to: Gas Heater Safety pillar

Gas Heater ──[competes with]────→ Electric Heater
  → Intersection: Gas Heater vs Electric Heater Pakistan — Full Comparison
  → Links to: Gas Heater Types pillar, Gas Heater Price pillar

Gas Heater ──[is supplied by]───→ SNGPL/SSGC
  → Intersection: Gas Heater Running Cost in Pakistan — SNGPL/SSGC Tariff Guide
  → Links to: Gas Heater Wattage pillar, Gas Heater Fuel Type pillar
```

### Source 5 — User Segment Specific Articles (Outer Section)

**Origin:** User group segmentation dimension from the Mind Map
**Rule:** When a specific user segment has documented query behaviour around the Central Entity that is not served by any existing Core article, a targeted Outer article serves that segment.
**Result:** Articles targeting specific user sub-groups — apartment dwellers, parents, elderly buyers, first-time buyers, upgraders.

```
User segment: Parents / safety-conscious buyers
  → Outer: Gas Heater Safety for Homes with Children in Pakistan
  → Links to: Gas Heater Safety pillar

User segment: Small apartment residents
  → Outer: Best Gas Heater for Small Apartments in Pakistan (Under 200 sq ft)
  → Links to: Gas Heater Wattage pillar, Gas Heater Types pillar

User segment: First-time buyers
  → Outer: First-Time Heater Buyer's Guide Pakistan — What to Know Before Buying
  → Links to: Gas Heater Types pillar, Gas Heater Brands pillar, Gas Heater Price pillar
```

## LESSON 7.5 — Topical Map Components: What Every Entry Must Contain

Each row in the Topical Map represents one article. Each row must be complete before the article brief is written. An incomplete topical map entry produces an incomplete brief which produces an incomplete article.

### The Eight Mandatory Components

**1. Topic**
The entity + attribute pair this article covers. Never a keyword. Always an entity-attribute relationship.

```
WRONG: "gas heater price"
RIGHT: Entity: Gas Heater | Attribute: Price | Context: Pakistan 2025
```

**2. Section Classification**
Core or Outer. This determines depth standard, publishing priority, and quality threshold.

**3. Parent CMap Page**
Which CMap page (from Module 6) this article sits beneath. Every article has exactly one parent. No orphan articles.

```
Article: Gas Heater Types Supporting Article (Infrared)
Parent CMap page: P-01 (Gas Heater Types Pillar — /heaters/gas-heater/types/)
```

**4. Title Tag**
The exact H1/title tag for this article, built using the 11-source title construction system from Module 3. Not a working title — the final title.

```
"Infrared Gas Heater in Pakistan — How It Works, Price, and Best Models"
```

**5. Meta Description**
150–160 characters. Contains: primary keyword entity-attribute, key value proposition, call to action or qualifier.

```
"Infrared gas heaters provide direct, instant warmth for Pakistani homes.
Compare models, prices (PKR 12,000–28,000), and brands including Gree and Orient."
[152 characters]
```

**6. URL**
The final URL for this article, inheriting from the parent CMap URL.

```
Parent: /heaters/gas-heater/types/
Article: /heaters/gas-heater/types/infrared-gas-heater/
```

**7. Primary and Secondary Keywords**
The primary keyword group (from Module 2 keyword grouping) this article serves, plus secondary keywords served within the same macro context.

```
Primary: infrared gas heater pakistan
Secondary: radiant gas heater, quartz gas heater, best infrared heater pakistan,
           infrared heater price pakistan
```

**8. Internal Link Targets**
Which CMap pages and Topical Map articles this article links to, with proposed anchor text. Planned before writing — not discovered during writing.

```
Outbound links:
→ Gas Heater Types Pillar (parent): "types of gas heaters in Pakistan"
→ Gas Heater Safety Pillar: "gas heater indoor safety"
→ Gas Heater Price Pillar: "gas heater price in Pakistan"
→ [Next supporting article]: "catalytic gas heater Pakistan"
```

### Additional Recommended Components

**9. Macro Context Statement**
One sentence defining what this article is about. Used in the brief to prevent scope creep.

```
"This article covers the infrared/radiant type of gas heater — how it produces
heat, its advantages and disadvantages, which Pakistani brands make it,
and its price range in PKR."
```

**10. Publish Date / Priority**
When this article should be published relative to others. Core articles first. Outer articles after Core is established. Supporting articles after parent pillar is live.

**11. Word Count Guidance**
Minimum necessary and maximum efficient based on attribute depth and competitor coverage. Not a target — a guardrail.

**12. Information Gain Opportunities**
The specific data points or attribute values from the Entity Map that competitors miss — carried forward from Module 4's information gain audit.

## LESSON 7.6 — Topic Prioritisation: What to Publish First

### The Fatal Mistake: Publishing in the Wrong Order

The most common topical map failure is publishing in the wrong order. The typical mistake:

1. Research a long list of topics
2. Pick the most "interesting" or "easiest" ones to write first
3. End up with 20 Outer section articles and 0 Core articles
4. Wonder why the site has no authority and nothing is ranking

**The correct order is not optional.** It is determined by the architecture, not by writer preference.

### The Four-Phase Publishing Sequence

**Phase 1 — Foundation (before anything else)**
CMap structural pages must be live: homepage, About Us, Contact, Privacy Policy, Ontology Root Page. No articles until these exist.

```
[ ] Homepage live
[ ] About Us live (with author + expertise signals)
[ ] Contact live (with physical address)
[ ] Privacy Policy live
[ ] Ontology Root Page live (/heaters/)
```

**Phase 2 — Core Pillars (first content wave)**
All Core section root attribute articles. These are the load-bearing articles. Published before any Outer section articles.

```
Publish order:
1. Gas Heater Types Pakistan (highest search volume + most authoritative)
2. Gas Heater Price Pakistan (highest commercial intent volume)
3. Gas Heater Brands Pakistan (highest comparison intent volume)
4. Gas Heater Wattage Guide Pakistan (highest education intent volume)
5. Gas Heater Safety Pakistan (highest informational anxiety volume)
6. Gas Heater Fuel Type / LPG vs Natural Gas (high informational volume)
```

Rationale for this order: The article with the highest search volume and most root-attribute coverage goes first. It establishes the strongest initial topicality signal. Each subsequent Core article reinforces the established context.

**Phase 3 — Deep Core Supporting Articles**
Value cluster expansion articles that sit beneath Phase 2 pillars. Published only after the parent pillar is indexed and has some initial performance data.

```
After "Gas Heater Types" pillar is live:
→ Blue Flame Gas Heater Pakistan
→ Infrared Gas Heater Pakistan
→ Catalytic Gas Heater Pakistan — Safest Indoor Option?

After "Gas Heater Brands" pillar is live:
→ Gree Gas Heater All Models Pakistan
→ Orient Gas Heater Review Pakistan
→ Dawlance Gas Heater Pakistan
```

**Phase 4 — Outer Section Articles**
Only after Phase 2 and Phase 3 have been published, indexed, and are showing performance signals. Outer articles expand the query surface area — but they need the Core to link to.

```
After Core is established:
→ Gas Heater for Small Apartments Pakistan
→ Gas Heater vs Oil-Filled Heater Pakistan
→ Gas Heater Carbon Monoxide Risk Guide
→ Gas Heater Maintenance Guide Pakistan
→ First-Time Heater Buyer Guide Pakistan
→ Gas Heater Running Cost SNGPL/SSGC Guide
```

### Priority Scoring Formula

For articles within the same phase, use this scoring formula to determine order:

```
Priority Score = (Search Volume × Intent Score) + Information Gain Bonus
                 ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                               Keyword Difficulty

Intent Score:
  Transactional = 3
  Commercial = 2
  Informational = 1

Information Gain Bonus:
  High (competitors miss 3+ attributes) = +20
  Medium (competitors miss 1–2 attributes) = +10
  Low (minimal gap) = +0
```

Higher priority score = published earlier within the same phase.

## LESSON 7.7 — Connecting the Topical Map to the CMap

Every article in the Topical Map is a child of a page in the CMap. This connection must be explicit — not assumed. Every row in the Topical Map spreadsheet has a `Parent CMap Page ID` column that references a specific entry in the CMap.

### The Parent-Child Relationship

```
CMap Entry: P-01 (Gas Heater Types Pillar — /heaters/gas-heater/types/)
  │
  ├── Topical Map Article TM-01
  │     Title: Blue Flame Gas Heater in Pakistan
  │     URL: /heaters/gas-heater/types/blue-flame-gas-heater/
  │     Parent: P-01
  │
  ├── Topical Map Article TM-02
  │     Title: Infrared Gas Heater Pakistan — How It Works + Price
  │     URL: /heaters/gas-heater/types/infrared-gas-heater/
  │     Parent: P-01
  │
  └── Topical Map Article TM-03
        Title: Catalytic Gas Heater — Pakistan's Safest Indoor Heater?
        URL: /heaters/gas-heater/types/catalytic-gas-heater/
        Parent: P-01
```

### What This Connection Means for Internal Linking

Every article links upward to its parent CMap page. Every CMap pillar page links downward to its child Topical Map articles. This bidirectional link structure creates the semantic cluster that Google uses to understand the relationship between these pages.

```
P-01 (Gas Heater Types Pillar)
  ├── Links DOWN to: TM-01, TM-02, TM-03 (using descriptive anchor text)
  │
TM-01 (Blue Flame)
  └── Links UP to: P-01 (parent pillar)
  └── Links ACROSS to: TM-02, TM-03 (sibling articles — same parent)
  └── Links UP to: C-01 (Ontology Root) — if directly relevant
```

This architecture means no article is ever isolated. Every article is connected to the semantic cluster of its parent, which is connected to the ontology root, which is connected to the homepage. No orphan pages. No disconnected content.

## LESSON 7.8 — The Complete Topical Map Spreadsheet

### Column Structure

```
Column A  — Article ID (TM-01, TM-02, etc.)
Column B  — Phase (1 Foundation / 2 Core Pillar / 3 Deep Core / 4 Outer)
Column C  — Section (Core / Outer)
Column D  — Topic (Entity + Attribute + Context)
Column E  — Article Source (Root Attribute / Value Cluster / Query Path /
                            Related Entity / User Segment)
Column F  — Parent CMap Page ID (references CMap document)
Column G  — Title Tag (final, not working title)
Column H  — URL (full path — inherits from CMap parent)
Column I  — Meta Description (150–160 chars)
Column J  — Primary Keyword
Column K  — Secondary Keywords (comma-separated)
Column L  — Search Intent
Column M  — Query Path Stage
Column N  — Macro Context Statement (1 sentence)
Column O  — Internal Links Out (Target URL + proposed anchor text)
Column P  — Information Gain Opportunities (from Entity Map audit)
Column Q  — TF-IDF Required Terms (top 10 from competitor analysis)
Column R  — Priority Score
Column S  — Publish Date (planned)
Column T  — Author Assignment
Column U  — Brief Status (Not Started / In Progress / Complete)
Column V  — Article Status (Not Started / In Progress / Review / Live)
Column W  — Notes
```

### Topical Map Completion Checklist

Before the Topical Map is handed to writers, verify:

```
TOPICAL MAP COMPLETION CHECKLIST
==================================

[ ] No two articles have identical macro context statements

Coverage:
[ ] All root attributes have at least one Core article
[ ] All Mind Map query overlays are covered by at least one article
[ ] All five article sources are represented (not all from one source)
[ ] All user groups from Mind Map have at least one corresponding article
[ ] All related entity connections have an intersection article

Prioritisation:
[ ] Phase classification is correct for all articles
[ ] Phase 1 (CMap structural pages) are already live
[ ] Phase 2 (Core pillars) are all present in the map
[ ] Phase 3 articles have Phase 2 parent verified live
[ ] Phase 4 articles are not scheduled before Phase 2+3 are complete

Quality signals:
[ ] Every title passes the 5-point title test from Module 3
[ ] Every URL is clean — no duplication, correct hierarchy, correct depth
[ ] Every meta description is 150–160 characters with entity + value + qualifier
[ ] Every article has a minimum of 2 documented outbound internal link targets
[ ] Every Core article has documented information gain opportunities
[ ] All TF-IDF required terms are populated

Internal linking consistency:
[ ] No two articles share identical anchor text pointing to different targets
[ ] Parent CMap pages have downward links to all child articles documented
[ ] Outer section articles have documented links back to at least one Core article
[ ] Sibling articles (same parent) have cross-links to each other documented
```

## MODULE 7 — SUMMARY

The Topical Map is the complete content allocation plan for every article the site will ever publish. It is not a content calendar. It is a structured architectural document that maps every query group to a specific page with a specific parent, URL, title, meta, and internal link plan — all before a single word of content is written.

**The Core/Outer distinction** governs every depth and priority decision. Core articles build authority. Outer articles expand coverage. Core always comes first.

**Topic derivation** from five documented sources ensures every article traces back to entity research or query data — nothing is invented, nothing is duplicated.

**The three topical map dimensions** — Vast, Depth, Momentum — define the strategic character of the site's content programme. The strategic rule is constant: never sacrifice depth for width, never sacrifice quality for speed.

**The parent-child architecture** connecting every Topical Map article to a CMap page ensures no orphan content, clean URL inheritance, and a functioning internal link structure before any writer opens a document.

**The four-phase publishing sequence** prevents the most common topical authority failure: Outer articles published before Core articles are established.

When the Topical Map is complete and its checklist passes, every writer receives two things: their topical map row and their content brief (Module 8). They write. They never plan.

## MODULE 7 — KEY TERMS GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Topical Map** | The complete allocation of every planned article to a specific page with defined macro context, URL, parent, and internal link structure. |
| **Conceptual Map** | A content plan built by imitating existing SERP coverage — produces average content with no differentiation. |
| **Core Section** | Articles covering root attributes of the Central Entity at maximum depth. Builds topical authority. |
| **Outer Section** | Articles covering peripheral attributes and user segments. Expands query surface. Links to Core. |
| **Vast (Width)** | How many distinct topics the Outer Section covers — the horizontal breadth of query coverage. |
| **Depth (Height)** | How many supporting article levels exist beneath each Core pillar — the vertical depth of authority. |
| **Momentum (Speed)** | The publication velocity of the topical map — how fast articles are published and the map is expanded. |
| **Phase publishing** | The mandatory four-phase sequence: Foundation → Core Pillars → Deep Core → Outer. |
| **Parent-child architecture** | The structural relationship between every Topical Map article and its parent CMap page. |
| **Priority score** | A numeric score derived from search volume, intent, keyword difficulty, and information gain — determines article publish order within each phase. |
| **Article source** | The documented origin of every article topic: Root Attribute, Value Cluster, Query Path, Related Entity, or User Segment. |
| **Macro context statement** | A one-sentence definition of exactly what a specific article covers — used to prevent scope creep in the brief. |

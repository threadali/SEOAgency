# SaaS SEO Course
## Module 13 — Execution Sequence: The Complete SaaS SEO Roadmap

**Prerequisite:** All modules complete. All deliverables signed off.
**Module outcome:** A phased SaaS SEO Execution Roadmap — every deliverable, every content type, and every measurement checkpoint in the correct sequence, from day one through to a compounding organic programme.

---

## Why Sequence Is the Course's Final Lesson

Every technique in this course produces its maximum output in the correct order. The same content, the same pages, and the same keyword architecture produce dramatically different results depending on when they are built relative to each other.

A comparison page published before the pricing page exists sends buyers to a dead end. A TOFU article cluster published before the BOFU and MOFU layer is established drives traffic with no conversion path. An integration page built before the template is validated scales failure. A retention knowledge base built on a subdomain loses every topical authority signal it generates.

The sequence is not theoretical. It is the production order that prevents all of these outcomes simultaneously.

---

## Lesson 13.1 — Stage 1: Foundation and BOFU First (Months 1-2)

Stage 1 builds what must exist before any content is published and captures the buyers who are closest to signing up.

**Week 1-2: Product Intelligence and Architecture**

Produce all foundational deliverables before a single page is built or a single keyword is researched.

```
WEEK 1-2 CHECKLIST
===================
[ ] Product Intelligence Document complete and signed off (Module 1)
[ ] Keyword Architecture Spreadsheet complete (Module 2)
    — All four layers mapped
    — Competitor keyword gap matrix complete
    — Integration keyword map complete
[ ] CMap complete and signed off (Module 3)
    — URL directory structure agreed
    — Domain structure decisions documented (subdomains vs subdirectories)
    — Crawl configuration checklist verified
[ ] GA4 configuration complete (Module 12)
    — Conversion events firing
    — Content groups created
    — Search Console integrated
```

Do not start content production until every item in this checklist is complete. Teams that skip foundational work and start with content production spend three to six months building a programme on a misaligned architecture that requires rework.

**Week 3-6: Build the Structural BOFU Layer**

Build every BOFU page in the brief set from Module 6 before publishing any blog content.

```
WEEK 3-6 PRIORITY ORDER
========================
Day 1:   Pricing page live (with title tag, meta, FAQ, social proof)
Day 3:   Free trial page live (with no-credit-card statement, trial contents)
Day 5:   Demo page live (with agenda, who it is for, 5-field form max)
Week 4:  Security page live (if B2B mid-market or enterprise)
Week 4:  ROI / business case page live (if B2B mid-market or enterprise)
Week 5:  Top 3 competitor "vs" pages live (from Module 7 priority matrix)
Week 6:  Top 3 "alternatives" pages live
```

Every page published here should be indexed before Stage 2 begins.

**Why this order matters**

BOFU pages with 90 days of indexing history rank faster for their target keywords than BOFU pages launched after a content programme is already running. Google's freshness and authority signals accumulate from first indexing date. Starting the BOFU layer in week one gives it three to six months of authority accumulation before MOFU content links to it.

An early-stage sales engagement SaaS with domain authority of 22 cannot realistically rank for "sales engagement software" against Outreach and Salesloft. But within 90 days of launching four BOFU pages, they can generate their first organic demo requests from comparison and alternative queries. The BOFU layer works before the TOFU programme has produced a single ranking.

---

**Common Mistake: Publishing one article while the BOFU layer is still being built**

One TOFU article published before the BOFU layer is complete sends traffic to a site with no clear conversion path. The buyer who finds the article has nowhere to go that moves them toward a signup. The weeks spent building that article should have been spent completing the BOFU layer that all future content will link to.

---

**Summary**

- Two weeks of foundational work before any content is published or any page is built.
- Six weeks to build the complete BOFU structural layer: pricing, trial, demo, security, ROI, top comparison and alternatives pages.
- BOFU pages begin accumulating indexing authority from week one. This is a compounding head start the programme benefits from for years.

## Lesson 13.2 — Stage 2: MOFU and First TOFU (Months 2-4)

Stage 2 builds the evaluation layer and the first TOFU content cluster after the BOFU layer is indexed and beginning to rank.

**Gate condition for entering Stage 2**

Before Stage 2 content begins:
- All BOFU pages are indexed (confirmed in Search Console URL Inspection)
- At least one BOFU page is generating organic impressions (confirmed in Search Console Performance)
- GA4 conversion events are confirmed firing on BOFU pages

If the BOFU layer is not yet indexed after six weeks, investigate technical issues (robots.txt, noindex tags, crawl budget problems) before adding more content.

**Stage 2 content priority order**

```
MONTH 2:
  [ ] Top 3 feature pages live (Module 5, Lesson 5.4)
  [ ] Top 3 use-case / industry pages live (Module 5, Lesson 5.5)
  [ ] Top 2 remaining competitor "vs" pages (from Module 7 priority matrix)
  [ ] Top 2 "alternatives" pages

MONTH 3:
  [ ] Integration hub live (/integrations/) (Module 3, Lesson 3.5)
  [ ] Top 5 individual integration pages live
  [ ] 2-3 remaining feature pages
  [ ] First TOFU pillar published (highest Business Potential Score cluster)

MONTH 4:
  [ ] First TOFU pillar indexed — first supporting articles begin
  [ ] Top 2 case studies published (Module 5, Lesson 5.6)
  [ ] Remaining high-priority integration pages
  [ ] Content audit of any existing blog content (Module 4, Lesson 4.6)
      — Fix and relaunch candidates updated with product placement and CTAs
```

**The content audit before new TOFU production**

If the client has existing blog content, run the five-category content audit from Module 4 before publishing new TOFU articles. Fix-and-relaunch candidates from the existing blog will start generating signups within 60 days, often before any new content produces its first ranking. This is the fastest signup impact available in Stage 2.

**Publishing cadence for Stage 2**

| Team size | Recommended Stage 2 cadence |
|---|---|
| Solo writer | 2 pages/week (prioritise MOFU — higher conversion value per hour of production) |
| 2-writer team | 3-4 pages/week (MOFU first, TOFU second) |
| Agency/dedicated team | 5-8 pages/week (MOFU and TOFU in parallel) |

Do not exceed cadence at the cost of brief compliance. One page published from a complete brief converts better than three pages published from incomplete briefs.

---

**Common Mistake: Starting a second TOFU cluster before the first is indexed**

The first TOFU pillar must be indexed and receiving impressions before supporting articles are published. The first cluster's pillar must be in the top 20 for its primary keyword before a second TOFU cluster begins. Publishing multiple clusters simultaneously before any are ranking dilutes authority on all of them.

---

**Summary**

- Gate condition for Stage 2: all BOFU pages indexed, at least one generating impressions.
- Content audit of existing blog before new TOFU production. Fix-and-relaunch produces signups faster than new content.
- Stage 2 cadence: MOFU first, TOFU second. One TOFU cluster at a time until the first is ranking.

## Lesson 13.3 — Stage 3: Scale and Compound (Months 4-12)

Stage 3 scales what is working and builds the long-term compounding layer. It begins only after the BOFU layer is generating signups and Stage 2 content is demonstrating ranking traction.

**Gate condition for entering Stage 3**

Before Stage 3 begins:
- At least one BOFU page is generating organic signups
- At least one MOFU page is in the top 10 for its primary keyword
- First TOFU pillar is ranking in the top 20

If these conditions are not met by month 4, do not scale. Diagnose the blocking issue first.

**Stage 3 production programme**

```
MONTHS 4-6:
  [ ] Programmatic page programme: validate first template
      — Build 3-5 integration pages manually, confirm ranking + conversion
      — Build 3-5 industry/use-case pages manually, confirm ranking + conversion
      — Template validated: [ ] Yes → scale approved
  [ ] TOFU: Second cluster begins (first cluster pillar in top 20)
  [ ] MOFU: Remaining comparison and alternatives pages
  [ ] Remaining feature pages
  [ ] Knowledge base / retention content begins on main domain
      — /docs/ or /help/ architecture built
      — First 10 retention articles published

MONTHS 6-9:
  [ ] Programmatic pages at scale (after template validated)
  [ ] TOFU: Third cluster (second cluster pillar in top 20)
  [ ] Retention content programme: 2-3 articles/week
  [ ] Competitor page maintenance: First quarterly review cycle
  [ ] MOFU pages: Update and enrich pages that are ranking 11-20

MONTHS 9-12:
  [ ] Full programmatic programme running
  [ ] TOFU at scale: 2-4 articles/week if conversion from TOFU is justified
  [ ] 90-day decision gate review: all content published before month 6
      — Performing: maintain
      — Needs enrichment: add information gain targets
      — Needs structural revision: fix title/meta or macro context
      — Needs consolidation: merge cannibalising pages
```

**The 7-month breakeven benchmark**

B2B SaaS content programmes typically reach breakeven — where organic signups produce revenue that exceeds the programme's monthly cost — at month 7. This varies by domain authority, competitive landscape, and production investment. Teams that publish for three months, see slow results, and stop miss the compounding effect entirely.

The compounding pattern: months 1-3 are foundation building with no meaningful organic signups. Months 4-6 see first organic signups as BOFU and MOFU pages begin ranking. Months 7-12 produce consistent, growing organic pipeline as the content cluster authority compounds. Month 12 onward: SEO becomes a predictable, lower-cost acquisition channel than paid, and new content ranks faster because domain authority is established.

---

**Common Mistake: Scaling production before conversion is confirmed**

Stage 3 is the phase where agencies sometimes over-produce in response to client pressure for volume. A client who is not yet seeing signups from existing content does not need more content — they need the existing content to rank and convert. Diagnose before scaling. If BOFU pages are not converting, fix the conversion architecture. If MOFU pages are not ranking, investigate domain authority and link profile. If TOFU content is not indexed, investigate crawl issues. Production volume is not the lever that fixes these problems.

---

**Summary**

- Gate condition for Stage 3: BOFU generating signups, MOFU in top 10, first TOFU pillar in top 20.
- The 7-month breakeven benchmark is real. Programmes that stop before month 7 miss the compounding effect.
- Diagnose before scaling. Volume fixes nothing that structural or conversion problems are causing.

## Lesson 13.4 — Stage 4: Retention and Category Authority (Months 9+)

Stage 4 builds the retention layer and expands into category authority. It runs in parallel with Stage 3 from month 9 onward.

**Retention content programme**

The knowledge base and retention content on the main domain (Module 11 concepts) serve two audiences: existing users searching for help, and prospects evaluating whether the product is learnable. Both audiences benefit from comprehensive, well-structured help documentation.

Retention content publishing sequence:
1. Most-searched product features first (from Search Console: what queries land on the app or docs subdomain currently?)
2. Integration workflow guides for the top five integrations
3. Onboarding and quick-start documentation
4. Advanced use-case documentation
5. Changelog and new feature content

**Category authority expansion**

By month 9, if the Core JTBD clusters are ranking well, the programme can expand to adjacent JTBDs from the Product Intelligence Document. The expansion rule from Module 1: establish authority on the central entity first, then expand to adjacent entities that share the Source Context.

For a scheduling SaaS: Core authority is employee scheduling. Adjacent entities are: time tracking (same buyers, adjacent job), payroll integration (same buyers, downstream need), and labour compliance (same buyers, regulatory concern). Each expansion follows the same four-stage sequence applied to the new entity.

---

**Summary**

- Stage 4 runs from month 9: retention content on main domain, category authority expansion.
- Expansion follows the same four-stage sequence applied to the new adjacent entity.
- Never expand before Core entity authority is established. Dilution is faster than expansion.

## Lesson 13.5 — Agency Execution: Roles, Handoffs, and QA

The SaaS SEO execution sequence requires five distinct roles. In a small agency, one person plays multiple roles. In a full-service agency, each role has a dedicated owner. The clarity of the handoff determines the quality of the output.

**The five roles**

| Role | Responsibilities | Deliverables owned |
|---|---|---|
| SEO Strategist | Keyword architecture, CMap, Topical Map, priority decisions | Modules 1-3 deliverables, Execution Roadmap |
| Brief Builder | Content briefs, product placement specification, internal link assignments | Module 4-6 briefs, MOFU brief templates |
| Subject Matter Writer | Executes briefs, writes to product placement spec | Articles, MOFU pages, BOFU pages |
| QA Reviewer | Applies the six-stage QA checklist from the Semantic SEO course | QA sign-off per page |
| Measurement Analyst | GA4 configuration, attribution reporting, client reports | Module 12 deliverables |

**The handoff sequence**

```
PRODUCTION HANDOFF FLOW
========================
Strategist builds keyword cluster + adds to Topical Map
    ↓
Brief Builder produces brief (all 20 components for TOFU, spec brief for MOFU/BOFU)
    ↓
Brief reviewed and approved by Strategist
    ↓
Brief assigned to Writer with deadline
    ↓
Writer submits first draft
    ↓
QA Reviewer runs six-stage checklist
    ↓
  → Pass: Approved for publishing with technical SEO sign-off
  → Fail (Critical/Significant): Returned to Writer with return note
    ↓
Strategist adds internal links to Internal Linking Map for new page
    ↓
Page published and indexed
    ↓
Measurement Analyst confirms conversion events firing on new page
    ↓
90-day monitoring begins
```

**The QA gate is non-negotiable**

Every page — TOFU, MOFU, BOFU, integration, comparison — passes through the six-stage QA checklist from the Semantic SEO course before publishing. SaaS SEO adds the product placement verification and the CTA specification verification as additional BOFU and MOFU-specific checks.

No page publishes without QA sign-off. This is the rule that prevents the quality decay that happens in scaled content production when volume pressure overrides standards.

**Common client pressure points and how to handle them**

| Client pressure | Agency response |
|---|---|
| "Can we publish faster?" | "We can increase cadence within the brief compliance standard. We cannot reduce brief compliance to increase cadence." |
| "Can we skip QA for this one?" | "The QA process exists to prevent the specific failures that reduce conversion rate. Skipping it saves 2 hours and costs signup performance." |
| "Why are we not doing more TOFU?" | "TOFU converts at 0.19%. MOFU converts at 7.5%+. We will expand TOFU once the MOFU layer is ranking and converting." |
| "The rankings are not moving fast enough" | "Month [N] is within the normal compounding timeline. The BOFU layer is generating [N] signups. Rankings follow conversion signals, not just content volume." |

---

**Summary**

- Five roles, clear deliverable ownership, documented handoff flow. Ambiguous handoffs produce quality failures at scale.
- The QA gate applies to every page type. No exceptions.
- Standard client pressure points have standard responses grounded in the data from this course. Prepare the responses before the questions arrive.

## Lesson 13.6 — The Ten Most Expensive SaaS SEO Mistakes

These are the ten errors that consistently destroy SaaS SEO programme performance. All ten have appeared in earlier modules. This lesson consolidates them as the final reference.

**1. Building TOFU before BOFU**
TOFU converts at 0.19%. BOFU converts at 8-20%. Every month spent building TOFU before BOFU is a month of conversion opportunity lost. Build BOFU first.

**2. Putting the knowledge base on a subdomain**
Help.product.com and docs.product.com lose all topical authority from retention content to the subdomain. Build the knowledge base at /docs/ or /help/ on the main domain.

**3. Skipping the Product Intelligence Document**
Keyword research without a Product Intelligence Document produces a topical map of competitor keywords, not buyer keywords. The PSF matrix, JTBD statements, and Business Potential Score filter are prerequisites for every subsequent decision.

**4. Using single-keyword volume to score competitor pages**
Competitor page opportunity is a stacked cluster of eight to ten intent variants. Single-query volume underestimates opportunity by a factor of three to five. Stack before scoring.

**5. Publishing comparison pages that are promotional instead of honest**
Promotional comparison pages lose buyer trust at the stage where trust is the only thing standing between the buyer and a signup. Honest comparison pages convert at 8-15%. Promotional ones convert at 3-5% or less.

**6. No product placement specification in TOFU briefs**
A TOFU brief without a product placement specification produces one of two outcomes: the writer ignores the product entirely, or mentions it awkwardly at the end. Both waste the article's conversion potential.

**7. Treating competitor pages as static content**
Comparison pages with outdated pricing or missing features actively harm conversion and create legal risk. Quarterly review is mandatory. Every competitor page has a named owner from the day it publishes.

**8. Setting a minimum volume floor on B2B keywords**
A 200-search/month keyword in B2B SaaS with a $20K ACV product is worth twelve customers per month at standard conversion rates. Filtering it out at 200 searches loses those twelve customers permanently.

**9. Reporting traffic as the primary KPI**
Traffic growth with flat signups means the content programme is building audience but not pipeline. The primary KPI is organic signups. Traffic explains why signups are moving. It does not prove the programme is working.

**10. Stopping before month 7**
The compounding effect is not visible in months 1-3. It becomes visible in months 4-6 and significant in months 7-12. Programmes that stop at month 3 because results are slow miss the entire return on the investment already made. Build a clear compounding timeline expectation into month one of every client engagement.

## Module 13 Summary and Course Conclusion

The SaaS SEO course has covered thirteen disciplines. Each one depends on the previous. The Product Intelligence Document makes keyword research relevant. Keyword research makes the CMap accurate. The CMap makes the Topical Map architectural. The Topical Map makes briefs precise. Precise briefs make content convert. Converting content justifies the programme. The programme that compounds pays for the next twelve months of investment from its own revenue.

The sequence is everything. Individual techniques applied without sequence produce the "traffic up, signups flat" outcome. The same techniques applied in the correct sequence produce the compounding programme that becomes a competitive moat.

**The production sequence in one view:**

```
COMPLETE SAAS SEO EXECUTION SEQUENCE
======================================

STAGE 1 (Months 1-2) — Foundation and BOFU
  Week 1-2:  Product Intelligence Document + Keyword Architecture + CMap + GA4
  Week 3-6:  Pricing, Trial, Demo, Security, ROI pages
             Top 3 "vs" pages + Top 3 "alternatives" pages
  Gate:      All BOFU pages indexed. GA4 conversion events firing.

STAGE 2 (Months 2-4) — MOFU and First TOFU
  Month 2:   Feature pages + Use-case pages + Remaining competitor pages
  Month 3:   Integration hub + Top 5 integration pages + First TOFU pillar
  Month 4:   TOFU cluster 1 supporting articles + Case studies + Content audit
  Gate:      BOFU generating signups. MOFU in top 10. TOFU pillar in top 20.

STAGE 3 (Months 4-12) — Scale
  Month 4-6: Programmatic template validation → scale if validated
             TOFU cluster 2 (cluster 1 pillar in top 20)
             Retention content begins on main domain
  Month 6-9: Programmatic at scale + TOFU at scale + quarterly competitor review
  Month 9-12: 90-day gate review on all content. Enrich, revise, or consolidate.
  Gate:      7-month breakeven. Organic CAC below paid CAC.

STAGE 4 (Months 9+) — Retention and Expansion
  Month 9+:  Full retention content programme
             Adjacent JTBD expansion
             Category authority building
```
